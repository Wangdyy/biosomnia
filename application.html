Application

Ligation

1. Employing the same restriction enzyme, COMT with pTetR and a plasmid DNA fragment can be cut and produce matching sticky ends.

2. The process of annealing, the joining of base-pairing will allow for the combination of the plasmid and LEGO proteins.

3. The fragments are bonded by enzyme DNA ligase, producing a molecule of recombinant DNA

Transformation and Cloning

1. The recombinant plasmid or molecular clone is introduced into an Escherichia coli cell.

2. The Escherichia coli cell is allowed to reproduce

3. The recombinant plasmid can be identified by its resistance to ampicillin from promoter

Extraction

1. Cells broken open by adding a mixture of lysozome and detergent to digest the outer layer of the cell wall and separates the fatty cell membrane.

2. The Escherichia coli DNA is treated with cyanogen bromide, splitting protein chains to separate the COMT with pTetR chains from remaining DNA.

3. The process of chromatography purifies COMT with pTetR.

Injection

1. COMT with pTetR can be transported directly into the bloodstream using a syringe.

2. Anhydrotetracycline is preferred in activating the gene, as it exhibits no antibiotic activity. It is also injected during the desired period of sleep.

3. Melatonin expression is maximized.
